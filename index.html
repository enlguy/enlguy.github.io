I started making choose your own adventure games when I was 10 years old with BASIC and a lot of if-then-else statements. I decided at about 12 years old I wanted to make computer games. I was probably the youngest person to ever check out a C++ book from our town's library. I also learned at that age why coding is divided by languages. It really was a foreign language.



I guess I've come a long ways from there, but I found web development in 2016 when I was asked to build a website for a professional organization because "I was the young one." I began learning coding in my free time, and since then have found it almost as an escape - a rabbit hole down which I can disappear for hours just trying to find the perfect css approach get something to look just right.



I still approach my work with a sense of wonder, adventure, and fun. I am passionate about building beautiful pages that accomplish a purpose, whether it's helping you convert leads, better engage with an audience, or just share cool stuff. I am highly resourceful and a self-starter (everything I know is self-taught, and all the work you see is unpaid and done for the sake of learning and making something). My portfolio is only limited by my recent entry to this world, but I would love to work with you, and show you what I'm capable of.
